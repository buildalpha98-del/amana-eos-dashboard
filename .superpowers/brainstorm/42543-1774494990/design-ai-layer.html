Design Section 2: AI Integration Layer

How questions become answers with document citations

Two Access Points

1. Existing Assistant — New Tool

Add search_knowledge_base to the 6 existing assistant tools in ai-tools.ts

When a user asks a policy/procedure question in the assistant chat, Claude decides to call this tool automatically — just like it calls lookup_financial_period for finance questions.

No UI changes needed. The assistant just gets smarter.

2. Dedicated /knowledge Page

Simple Q&A interface — type a question, get an answer with source citations.

Uses a dedicated POST /api/knowledge/ask route that does the search + Claude call in one step (no tool-use loop needed).

Simpler, faster, purpose-built for "what does our policy say about X?"

New AI Prompt Template: knowledge/answer

Prompt Structure
You are a knowledge assistant for Amana OSHC staff. Answer questions using ONLY the provided document excerpts. RULES: - Cite your sources: "According to [Document Title], Section [heading]..." - If the excerpts contain a clear answer, provide it confidently - If the excerpts are partially relevant, answer what you can and flag what's uncertain with ⚠️ - If no excerpts are relevant, say: "I couldn't find this in your uploaded documents" and suggest general guidance with a clear "⚠️ Not from your documents" label - Use Australian English - Be concise and practical — staff need quick answers DOCUMENT EXCERPTS: {{chunks}} QUESTION: {{question}}

Response Format

Example: "What's our sun safety policy?"

According to your Sun Safety Policy (Section 2 — Outdoor Play Requirements):

  • Children must wear SPF 50+ sunscreen, applied 20 minutes before outdoor play
  • Broad-brimmed hats are required — no caps or visors
  • Outdoor activities are restricted between 11am–3pm from September to April
  • Educators must model sun-safe behaviour
📄 Sources: Sun Safety Policy (uploaded 12 Mar 2026) — chunks 3, 4
Example: Low confidence — "Do we accept crypto payments?"

I couldn't find information about cryptocurrency payments in your uploaded documents.

⚠️ Not from your documents: Most Australian OSHC services accept direct debit, EFTPOS, and CCS subsidy payments. For payment method queries, check with your accounts team or refer to your Fee Schedule.

⚠️ No matching documents found
