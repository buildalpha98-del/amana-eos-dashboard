The Role of an Amana OSHC Educator

Purpose

  • Support children's wellbeing, learning and development through high-quality, play-based programs
  • Work collaboratively under the direction of the OSHC Coordinator / Educational Leader to deliver safe, inclusive and engaging care

Core Responsibilities

  • Build positive, respectful relationships with children, families, and colleagues
  • Implement the My Time Our Place (MTOP) framework in daily practice
  • Assist with planning, delivering and evaluating educational and leisure experiences
  • Uphold child-safe standards and mandatory reporting requirements
  • Maintain confidentiality, professionalism and compliance with all Amana OSHC policies, the National Law & Regulations, NQS & WHS Acts

Key Qualities

Nurturing, observant, culturally aware, proactive and reflective in practice.

Key Focus Areas & Expectations

1. Educational Program & Practice

Create inspiring play-based environments that extend children's learning, document progress and reflect on outcomes.

2. Children's Health & Safety

Ensure constant supervision, safe ratios, first-aid readiness, hygiene, and adherence to food-safety and child-protection procedures.

3. Physical Environment

Maintain safe, stimulating and sustainable indoor/outdoor areas; report hazards and equipment issues promptly.

4. Professional Conduct & Teamwork

Model ethical behaviour, follow the ECA Code of Ethics, participate in meetings, and contribute to continuous improvement (SAT/QIP).

5. Relationships & Community

Promote inclusion, cultural safety and partnerships with families and local communities to strengthen children's sense of belonging.

The purpose of the Child Safe Standards is to ensure policies are implemented into daily practice and children's safety is everyone's priority. In complying with the standards our services must be promoting cultural safety for all children — including Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander children, and children with a disability.

Educator Responsibilities

  • Report any suspicion of child abuse to the appropriate agency (with support from your Area Manager)
Legal Obligation: Amana OSHC staff will report any signs, conversations and behaviour that may compromise the health and wellbeing of a child to the necessary authority. Failure to report carries heavy fines, civil suit and jail time.

Indicators of Abuse

Educators must be able to recognise both behavioural and physical indicators of abuse. The following are examples — not exhaustive lists.

Physical Abuse

BEHAVIOURAL INDICATORS
  • Disclosure of injury inflicted by someone else
  • Unusual fear of physical contact with adults
  • Aggressive or disproportionate behaviour
  • Wearing clothes unsuitable for weather to hide injuries
  • Reluctance to go home; wary of parent/guardian
  • Habitual unexplained absences from school
PHYSICAL INDICATORS
  • Bruises or welts in unusual locations or configurations
  • Burns from objects (iron, cigarette, hot water)
  • Fractures inconsistent with the child's age or explanation
  • Multiple injuries, old and new
  • Effects of poisoning; internal injuries

Emotional Abuse

BEHAVIOURAL INDICATORS
  • Overly compliant or extremely demanding behaviour
  • Poor self-image, unexplained mood swings, self-harm
  • Behaviours not age-appropriate
  • Violent drawings or writing
  • Lack of positive social contact with other children
PHYSICAL INDICATORS
  • Language delay, stuttering or selective mutism
  • Delays in emotional, mental or physical development

Neglect

BEHAVIOURAL INDICATORS
  • Gorging when food is available; begging or stealing food
  • Withdrawn, listless, pale and weak
  • Little positive interaction with parent/carer
  • Poor or irregular non-attendance
  • Refusal or reluctance to go home
  • Self-destructive behaviour
PHYSICAL INDICATORS
  • Consistently dirty or inappropriately dressed
  • Consistently hungry, tired and listless
  • Unattended health problems
  • Inadequate shelter or unsafe conditions

Late/non-arrival procedures and authorisation to collect children

Late/Non-Arrival Procedure

Educators must follow the Keeping Children Safe: Non-arrival/missing child process where a child has not arrived at care and cannot be located. This document is located in your OSHC office or operations policy manual.

3:15
Safe arrival time — once the bell has gone, all booked children should be present and signed in
3:25
Make a PA announcement and/or contact the school office. Call and text parent to confirm the child is attending or has been collected
3:30
Continue to contact parent and all emergency contacts. Contact Area Manager
3:40
CALL EMERGENCY 000
For Amana OSHC Educators: Child safety is our priority. Ensure ALL children are accounted for.
For Families: ALWAYS mark "not attending" — even if it's last minute.

Authorisation to Collect

  • Only release a child to a person listed as authorised on their enrolment form (primary parent/guardian or emergency contact with permission to collect)
  • An emergency contact must produce photo ID and must be over 18 years of age — take a photocopy and add to the child's enrolment form
  • In the event an emergency contact is collecting, you must have prior communication with the parent to confirm their unavailability
  • If a parent/carer appears impaired (e.g. intoxicated), do not release the child — contact the Centre Director immediately
  • Never allow an unauthorised person to take a child, regardless of any verbal instruction
Never release a child to an unauthorised person under any circumstances.

Medical & Incidents

Medical conditions, medication administration, injuries and reportable incidents

Medical Conditions

A Risk Minimisation Plan must be developed for each child with a medical condition. The parent must sign off once they approve the risk minimisation actions. A copy of the medical action plan and risk minimisation plan must be:

  • In the child's enrolment form
  • In the medical folder
  • With the medication
Expired Medications: When a medication expires, contact the parent immediately. A replacement must be provided before the child attends again. Expired medications MUST be returned to the parent to dispose of.
Remember: The Coordinator can refuse care where medical documentation and medication has not been provided. At no point should there be children with medical conditions at the service without a current action plan, risk minimisation plan and medication.

Administration of Medication

  • Parents/guardians must sign the medication authorisation form allowing educators permission to administer medication
  • Children are not to self-medicate while in attendance
  • Medication must be stored as recommended by the package and away from other children
  • Medication must NOT be kept in the child's school bag
  • All medication must be in the original package/container, in date, with child's full name and a prescribed dosage
  • Only the prescribed dosage must be administered
  • When administering medication, two Educators must be present and both must sign the medication authorisation form
  • On collection, the parent/guardian must acknowledge the administration and provide a signature

Minor Incidents, Accidents & Injuries

  • Any time an educator performs First Aid (including cuts, bruising and abrasions), an Amana OSHC injury/accident/incident/trauma/illness report must be completed
  • Upon parent arrival, inform the parent and have them sign the report (can be signed via OWNA)
  • File the completed report with the child's enrolment form on OWNA
  • Head injuries must be reported to the Area Manager and the parent must be informed immediately regardless of severity
  • If an incident occurs at Before School Care, inform the school when releasing the child

Reportable Incidents

The following must be reported to the Regulatory Authority within 24 hours:
  • An emergency service attends the service
  • A child seeks medical attention for an injury that occurred at the service
  • Broken/fracture bones
  • Head injury requiring medical attention
  • Child has not arrived at care (non-arrival/missing child)
  • Child cannot be accounted for during care for longer than 5 minutes
  • Loss of a child
  • Child locked in or out of the service
  • Child walked off school premises or licenced space
  • Child has an asthma attack or anaphylactic reaction where an Epi-pen has been administered
  • The death of a child

Policies

All Policies — Quick Reference

A simplified summary of all 14 Amana OSHC policies and what you must do as an educator

1. Behaviour Guidance Policy

This policy guides how educators support children to develop self-regulation, manage conflict and build positive social skills — using encouragement and natural consequences rather than punishment.

As an Educator, you must:

  • Use positive language and redirection to guide behaviour; avoid shouting, threats or physical punishment
  • Model respectful behaviour and calm communication at all times
  • Involve children in setting fair group agreements and revisit them regularly
  • Document and report persistent or serious behaviour concerns to the Centre Director
  • Never use isolation, humiliation or food as a behaviour management tool

2. Child Protection Policy

This policy sets out our legal and ethical obligations to protect every child from abuse, neglect and harm — and our mandatory reporting responsibilities under NSW law.

As an Educator, you must:

  • Complete your mandatory reporter training and keep it current
  • Report any suspicion of child abuse or neglect to the Centre Director immediately — do not investigate yourself
  • If you have a reasonable belief a child is at risk, a report to the Child Protection Helpline (132 111) is required and cannot be withheld
  • Never disclose to a parent/carer that a report has been made if doing so could place a child at further risk
  • Document all observations, disclosures and actions factually and promptly in the incident log

3. Child Safe Environment Policy

This policy ensures all staff create and maintain an environment where children are physically and emotionally safe, and where their rights and voices are respected.

As an Educator, you must:

  • Actively supervise children at all times according to required ratios
  • Complete all required child safe training, including the Child Safe Standards modules
  • Report any unsafe physical environment (broken equipment, hazards) to the Centre Director
  • Foster a culture where children feel heard and are encouraged to speak up
  • Never be alone with a single child behind closed doors

4. Code of Conduct Policy

This policy outlines the professional standards and behaviours expected of all Amana staff — with children, families, colleagues and the broader community.

As an Educator, you must:

  • Treat all children, families and colleagues with respect, dignity and cultural sensitivity
  • Maintain professional boundaries — do not share your personal contact details with families or connect with them on personal social media
  • Dress in your Amana uniform and present professionally at all times
  • Do not discuss confidential information about children, families or colleagues outside of work
  • Report any breach of the Code of Conduct (by yourself or others) to the Centre Director

5. Dealing with Complaints Policy

This policy describes how families and staff can raise concerns, and how Amana responds — ensuring all complaints are handled fairly, promptly and confidentially.

As an Educator, you must:

  • If a family raises a concern with you, listen respectfully, acknowledge their feelings and refer them to the Centre Director if you cannot resolve it immediately
  • Do not dismiss, minimise or argue with a complainant
  • Document all complaints in writing, including the date, issue and actions taken
  • Never discuss a complaint with other families or staff who are not directly involved
  • Follow up to ensure complaints are resolved and the family has been informed of the outcome

6. Delivery and Collection of Children Policy

This policy ensures children are only released to authorised persons and that all arrivals and departures are accurately recorded.

As an Educator, you must:

  • Only release a child to a person listed as authorised on their enrolment record — check ID if the person is unfamiliar to you
  • Record every arrival and departure in OWNA at the time it occurs; never backfill
  • If a child is not collected by close, follow the Late/Non-Collection procedure and notify the Centre Director
  • If a parent/carer appears impaired (e.g. intoxicated), do not release the child — contact the Centre Director immediately
  • Never allow an unauthorised person to take a child, regardless of any verbal instruction

7. Educational Program Policy

This policy ensures our program is planned, documented and evaluated against the MTOP Framework and NQF, with each child's learning and development at the centre.

As an Educator, you must:

  • Contribute to program planning by observing and documenting individual children's interests and strengths
  • Ensure activities are intentional, inclusive and aligned to the My Time, Our Place framework
  • Record observations and evaluations in OWNA as directed
  • Invite children's voices and choices into daily activities
  • Reflect on the program's effectiveness in team meetings and planning sessions

8. Emergency and Evacuation Policy

This policy describes how staff must respond to emergencies — including fire, lockdown, medical events and natural disasters — to keep children and staff safe.

As an Educator, you must:

  • Know the location of all emergency exits, assembly points and the Emergency Management Plan at your site
  • Respond immediately and calmly to any alarm or emergency — do not wait for instructions
  • Account for every child using the OWNA roll during any evacuation; never leave until a headcount is confirmed
  • Call 000 for life-threatening emergencies and notify the Centre Director as soon as it is safe to do so
  • Participate in all required emergency drills and debriefs

9. Administration of First Aid Policy

This policy sets out how and when first aid is to be administered, and how incidents are to be recorded — ensuring children receive prompt, appropriate care.

As an Educator, you must:

  • Maintain a current First Aid, CPR and Anaphylaxis management certificate at all times
  • Administer first aid promptly and within the limits of your training; call 000 for serious injuries or medical emergencies
  • Record every first aid event in OWNA immediately, including the injury, treatment and any follow-up
  • Notify the Centre Director of all first aid events on the day they occur
  • Administer prescribed medication only when a signed medication authority is on file — never administer unauthorised medication

10. Privacy and Confidentiality Policy

This policy protects the personal and sensitive information of children, families and staff — and explains how we collect, store and use that information responsibly.

As an Educator, you must:

  • Never share a child's or family's personal information with unauthorised people, including other families
  • Do not photograph children without prior parental consent on file; never share images on personal devices or social media
  • Keep physical records secure and digital records password protected
  • If you become aware of a privacy breach, notify the Centre Director immediately
  • Access only the information you need to do your job; do not browse records out of personal curiosity

11. Safe Arrival of Children Policy

This policy ensures every child's arrival is confirmed and tracked — so we can quickly identify and respond if a child does not arrive as expected.

As an Educator, you must:

  • Mark every child present in OWNA as soon as they arrive; do not mark them present before they physically arrive
  • If a child who is booked does not arrive, contact the family within 15 minutes to confirm their whereabouts
  • Escalate to the Centre Director if a child cannot be accounted for
  • Record any safe-arrival call or action taken in OWNA
  • Never assume a child is absent without confirmation from the family or school

12. Safe Use of Digital Technologies Policy

This policy guides the safe, ethical and age-appropriate use of digital devices and online platforms by children in our care and by staff in the workplace.

As an Educator, you must:

  • Supervise children's use of devices and the internet at all times
  • Only use approved, age-appropriate digital resources and applications
  • Do not use your personal mobile phone for personal use while supervising children
  • Report any concerning online behaviour or content to the Centre Director immediately
  • Never allow children to access social media, chat platforms or unfiltered internet

13. Sick Leave Policy

This policy outlines the entitlements and process for taking personal/sick leave — and reminds staff of their responsibility to keep unwell children and staff away from the service.

As an Educator, you must:

  • Notify your Centre Director as early as possible (before your shift start) if you are unwell and cannot attend
  • Do not attend work if you have a contagious illness — you must be symptom-free for 24 hours before returning
  • Provide a medical certificate for absences of 3 or more consecutive days, or as requested
  • Advise families not to bring unwell children; follow exclusion period guidelines for communicable diseases
  • Record your leave accurately through the OWNA staff module

14. Work Health and Safety (WHS) Policy

This policy reflects Amana's commitment to providing a safe and healthy workplace — and each educator's responsibility to actively participate in keeping it that way.

As an Educator, you must:

  • Report all hazards, near-misses and incidents to the Centre Director on the day they occur and record in OWNA
  • Follow all safe work procedures and use equipment as instructed
  • Do not lift more than is safely manageable; use correct manual handling techniques
  • Participate in WHS training, inductions and risk assessments
  • Raise any WHS concerns with the Centre Director or Health and Safety Representative — you will not face any negative consequence for doing so
You have the right to raise safety concerns without fear of reprisal.
